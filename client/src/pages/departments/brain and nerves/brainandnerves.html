Neuropsychiatric Medicine

The Neuropsychiatric Medicine department addresses a range of psychological disorders caused by neurological brain abnormalities or psychosocial factors. Additionally, we strive to treat psychologically afflicted patients experiencing physical complications or organic disorders. Our department provides exceptional care for those suffering from mental and neurological illnesses.


Diagnosis and Treatment Policy

We offer comprehensive biological and psychosocial assessments, aiming for the mental well-being of patients through care including pharmacological treatment, modified electroconvulsive therapy, psychological and social support, vocational therapy, group therapy, and social work.


Specializations

*Schizophrenia, mood disorders, psychological disorders and aging, epilepsy

*Developmental disorders such as autism and attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD)

Diagnosis and treatment of the early stage of schizophrenia

Diagnosis and treatment of panic disorder and anxiety disorders

Psychiatric care for patients with physical disorders

Conditions We Address


*Mental disorders such as: (schizophrenia, bipolar mood disorders, and depression), *Organic psychiatric disorders such as: (aging and epilepsy),

*Neurological disorders such as anxiety disorders, panic disorder, stress-related disorders, developmental disorders like autism and hyperactivity

A stroke occurs when blood flow to a part of the brain is interrupted due to a blockage in blood flow or when there is bleeding due to the bursting of blood vessels.

If a patient is experiencing a stroke, they may suffer from weakness or paralysis in the arm and leg, difficulty in speech, loss of vision in part of the visual field, or complain of numbness or tingling on one side of the body. It is essential to seek urgent medical consultation in case of any of these events by calling 16010.

The Stroke Unit at Egypt International Hospital provides optimal care for a wide range of issues that can be caused by stroke or neurological events.

Patient rooms have space for families and visitors who are always welcomed, as we believe they are an important part of the support network for stroke patients.

The Stroke Unit at Egypt International Hospital is an ideal place for families to wait while the patient receives recurrent treatments and examinations from consultants, nursing staff, physiotherapists, occupational therapists, and other members of our specialized team.

Our physical therapy unit has a specialized team in stroke and neurological rehabilitation. With a fully equipped gym and treatment areas, therapy is available for all patients in both the inpatient ward and the outpatient clinic. Treatments are also available at home after discharge.

The Stroke Unit has access to a full range of services available at Egypt International Hospital, including state-of-the-art imaging with the latest MRI and CT scanners, comprehensive non-invasive cardiac testing, rapid blood tests, and more.

The Stroke Unit is a specialized unit committed to providing the most effective and successful forms of treatment for patient rehabilitation. While the treatment is tailored to the individual patient, our unique range of services is conveniently located under one roof, making the entire process more comfortable for patients.

Contact us if you would like to take a tour of the Stroke Unit, so you can see the benefits of coming to a world-class private hospital for stroke care and rehabilitation.

Neurophysiology

Neurophysiology involves testing the electrical functions of the brain, spinal cord, nerves in the limbs, and muscles. At the International Hospital Egypt, there are two methods for doing this: EMG for measuring muscle electrical activity and EEG for measuring brain electrical activity.


EMG & Nerve Conduction Study (NCS)

These tests assess the function of the nervous system, particularly in neurophysiological areas such as carpal tunnel syndrome and nerve injuries. They also provide confirmatory evidence of nerve root lesions (cervical and lumbar radiculopathy) and muscle disorders.


Electroencephalogram (EEG)

This test evaluates brain functions and is highly useful in diagnosing common neurophysiological disorders such as epilepsy, dementia, coma patients, and assessing brain functions after head injuries and cerebral oxygen deficiency.
