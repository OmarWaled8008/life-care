Heart Diseases :

The Cardiology Department at Egypt International Hospital offers cutting-edge innovations in heart care for patients of all ages. We provide medical solutions for various heart and circulatory system-related diseases, including heart failure, congenital heart diseases, and other disorders.

Our experienced cardiologists and advanced technology ensure high-quality and affordable care. Diagnostic tests evaluate different heart problems, and our Electrophysiology Lab offers advanced services for irregular heartbeats.

Egypt International Hospital excels in surgical and medical solutions for heart, lung, and vascular diseases across all age groups, from newborns to the elderly.

As a center of excellence, we attract top surgical and medical professionals, working as a unified team to provide the highest standards of care for patients with heart or lung diseases.

Heart and Chest Surgery Services :

Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG) :

Surgery for coronary artery bypass grafting can be performed on a beating heart or a stopped heart using a CABG pump. Both methods are routinely used based on the patient's needs.

Heart and Valves Surgery :

Modern and high-quality techniques are utilized for repairing valves across various types of coronary valve diseases.

Valve replacement options include mechanical valves or tissue valves for patients with severe conditions.

Cardiovascular Vessel Surgery :

Cardiovascular vessel surgery employs different patching techniques to address coronary artery diseases, covering vessel dilation and aortic anatomy.

Heart Failure Surgery :

Coronary artery surgery is considered a high-risk procedure.

Options include coronary restoration or replacement while preserving collateral circulation.

Ventricular reshaping surgery and vascular dilation in the left ventricle are also performed.

Cardiac Arrhythmia Surgery :

Our team for your child's heart care includes pediatric cardiac surgeons, cardiologists, cardiac anesthesiologists, and cardiovascular nurses - all specifically trained in treating heart diseases in children. We collaborate with other departments (pediatrics, neonatology, prenatal care, and physical therapy).

Our Heart Center is dedicated to providing top-rated cardiac care for all patients. From your first visit, you'll work with a team of professionals committed to supporting all the medical, physical, and emotional needs of your child.

Cardiac Catheterization Units:

Our catheterization unit features state-of-the-art vascular imaging machines known throughout the healthcare industry for superior image quality, low radiation dose for both patients and staff, and cost-effective procedures. The catheterization unit is a lab that performs tests and procedures assessing heart efficiency. During a cardiac catheterization, a catheter (a flexible tube inserted through a narrow opening in the body cavity to remove fluids) is introduced—procedures and tests include Electrocardiogram (ECG or EKG), angiography (using X-rays to find blockages in arteries), and implantation of cardiac rhythm management devices. Unlike operations performed in the operating room, you will be awake throughout the procedure.

Services:

*Preparation:

Prior to entering the catheterization unit, the doctor will explain the steps of the procedure, and you will need to sign a consent form. Then, an echo device will be connected to measure your heart rhythm. If you request to sleep during the procedure, general anesthesia will be administered to you.

*The Procedure:

During the procedure, the skin at the thigh, wrist, or neck will be cleansed with a special solution, then a local anesthetic will be given, and a catheter will be inserted using a specific vein or artery leading to your heart. The catheter is guided with the help of X-rays.

*After the Procedure:

Once the procedure is complete, the catheter is removed. Depending on the operation, you will be asked to rest for a short period. In cases of vascular repair, removal, or implantation of a rhythm management device, you may leave the hospital on the same day. In case of vascular repair for a heart attack, the hospital stay duration will be longer.

*Vascular Imaging:

Vascular imaging uses a special dye and a camera to capture images of blood flow in the artery or vein. This dye is passed through the catheter, and after several X-rays, the dye will identify areas where the arteries are blocked on the X-ray.

Vascular Repair Procedure:

Vascular repair is an intravascular procedure used to open obstructed parts of the artery. At one end of the catheter, there is a balloon that is slowly inflated to press the fatty deposits against the artery wall, allowing blood to flow smoothly through the arteries. If necessary, a stent may be inserted to reinforce the artery walls.
After surgery, you should eat and drink liquids to flush the dye out of your body. Depending on the type of surgery you had, the hospital discharge date will be determined. There are no after-effects of cardiac catheterization, and it is a regularly performed safe practice.
